# Diagnosis Rubric

Use this reference when the user asks what is weak, where a chapter breaks, or how a manuscript can be improved.

## Diagnostic Priority

Use priority levels:

- `P0`: breaks reader trust, blocks continuation, contradicts established facts, or makes the main action unbelievable.
- `P1`: weakens tension, motivation, causality, pacing, scene function, or character credibility.
- `P2`: line-level polish, paragraph rhythm, sensory detail, wording, or local clarity.

## Chapter Checks

Check only what is relevant to the user's request:

- **Purpose**: What must this chapter accomplish?
- **Reader promise**: What expectation does it set or pay off?
- **Causality**: Do events follow from choices, constraints, and consequences?
- **Character agency**: Does the protagonist act from desire, fear, plan, or pressure rather than author convenience?
- **Motivation visibility**: Can readers understand why each important choice is made?
- **Conflict pressure**: Is there an active obstacle, cost, deadline, or competing desire?
- **Scene necessity**: Does each scene change information, emotion, power, relationship, or stakes?
- **Information density**: Are rules and exposition placed near decisions that need them?
- **Voice**: Does dialogue, interiority, and narration fit the character and genre?
- **Hook**: Does the ending create a specific next question or pressure?
- **Subscription risk**: Would a serial reader feel the chapter was too easy, stalled, confusing, or not worth the next tap?

## Web-Novel Specific Checks

For serial or web-novel work, also check:

- protagonist success must create a new obligation, suspicion, cost, or enemy response
- antagonists and organizations should remain competent
- power/system rules should be dramatized through use, not dumped early
- chapter endings should not merely announce the next event
- genre promise should appear in each chapter without flattening character logic
- side characters should not help too quickly without their own motive
- major payoffs should meet resistance before reward
- victories should leave an aftertaste: cost, clue, debt, suspicion, injury, or next pressure
- the last beat should drive reader return with a concrete unresolved question

## Diagnosis Output

```markdown
## Overall Diagnosis
- Main issue:
- What is already working:

## Priority Issues
| Priority | Issue | Evidence | Reader Impact | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## What Not To Change
- ...

## Recommended Next Pass
- diagnosis only | light refinement | structural edit | continuity audit
```

## Evidence Rule

Attach evidence to serious claims. If exact line numbers are unavailable, cite chapter, scene, beat, or quoted short phrase. Do not give generic advice unless it is tied to this manuscript.

## Author-Friendly Summary

For web-novel authors, translate the diagnosis into practical reading impact:

- where readers may stop trusting the story
- where payoff arrives without enough resistance
- where the chapter may fail to drive the next click
- which fix gives the most value without changing the outline
