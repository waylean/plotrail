# Anti-AI Prose

Use this reference for every correction, polish, light revision, style calibration, or explicit "去AI味" request.

## Core Rule

Improve story function before sentence shine. Do not make the prose merely smoother, more symmetrical, more emotional, or more literary. Preserve the author's voice, event sequence, genre promise, and useful rough edges.

Do not imitate a named living author's distinctive style. Use admired writers only as high-level craft anchors.

## Craft Anchors From Strong Web-Novel Practice

These anchors are derived from public discussion and author interviews, not from copying prose:

- **Rhythm over ornament**: A readable web-novel chapter needs enough technique and pacing to keep readers moving. Avoid piling up "beautiful" phrases when a scene needs pressure, choice, or consequence.
- **Technical problem-solving**: Treat writing as solving specific craft problems: how to reveal information, when to withhold, how to make a victory cost something, and how to create a next-chapter question.
- **Freshness and lived material**: Prefer concrete situation, profession, place, procedure, object, or social pressure over generic atmosphere. If the world feels thin, add evidence, not adjectives.
- **Hard-edged character pressure**: Let people show desire, status, competence, and fear through action, friction, and dialogue. Avoid explaining every emotion.
- **Emotional restraint**: Let feeling arrive through what the character notices, refuses to say, or chooses under pressure.

## Common AI Flavor

Remove or reduce:

- neat three-part emotional summaries
- every paragraph ending with a thesis sentence
- repeated "仿佛/似乎/像是某种..." mood padding
- characters saying exactly what the scene already shows
- generic abstract nouns such as 命运, 使命, 真相, 黑暗, 光芒 when no concrete action supports them
- over-polished transitions that remove tension
- symmetrical paragraphs with the same rhythm
- motivational slogans after every choice
- explaining subtext immediately after dialogue
- instant mastery, instant realization, or instant emotional resolution

## Better Replacement Moves

Prefer:

- Replace explanation with a visible action, object, delay, test, wound, trace, record, or cost.
- Replace "he understood everything" with the exact piece of information he now has and what remains missing.
- Replace broad emotion with one physical behavior, one withheld sentence, or one changed decision.
- Replace generic tension with an active obstacle, deadline, witness, system check, or irreversible consequence.
- Replace a vague hook with a concrete new pressure or contradiction.
- Keep one sharp sentence instead of three polished sentences that say the same thing.

## Calibration Order

Use this order when multiple style sources exist:

1. User's original voice and stated intent.
2. Approved author editing profile or style snapshot.
3. Genre and platform needs.
4. User-provided craft anchors from admired writers.
5. General polish.

If these conflict, preserve author voice and story logic over external style anchors.

## Output For Anti-AI Pass

When the user asks to remove AI flavor, include:

```markdown
## Anti-AI Pass
- Removed:
- Preserved:
- Strengthened:
- Still risky:
```

For large chapters, revise a full section and provide a queue for the rest instead of pretending the whole chapter was fixed.

## External Craft Anchors

When using named writers as reference points, say:

```text
I will not imitate the writer's style. I will use this as a craft anchor for pacing / restraint / dialogue pressure / information release / texture.
```

Never generate "write this like <living author>" instructions. Reframe them as technique constraints.
