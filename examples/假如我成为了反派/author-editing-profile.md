# Author Editing Profile

This sample profile is distilled from the first 11 chapters and the edit analysis for `假如我成为了反派`.

## Keep

- Concrete investigation before exposition.
- Protagonist agency through procedure, constraint, test, fallback, and cost.
- Antagonists who stay competent even when the protagonist advances.
- Chapter endings that create a precise next-chapter pressure.
- Rules explained only when they change a decision.
- Moral cost after taking power from another body.

## Avoid

- Letting the protagonist win because the plot needs him to win.
- Solving a problem with unearned insight.
- Making organizations passive once the protagonist acts.
- Explaining rules before the reader needs them.
- Ending chapters with vague atmosphere instead of a concrete new pressure.

## Drafting Requirements

Every major choice should show:

1. a concrete reason
2. a visible risk
3. a procedural action
4. a fallback or constraint
5. a cost or unresolved consequence

## Review Checklist

- Does the chapter preserve approved canon?
- Does every reveal respect the current knowledge state?
- Does the protagonist's success create a new obligation, suspicion, or cost?
- Does the antagonist remain dangerous after the scene?
- Does the final beat pull the reader into the next chapter?
