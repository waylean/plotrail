# Sample Continuity Review

This sample shows the kind of review PlotRail writes after a chapter draft.

## Contract Check

| Item | Status | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Communication weakness is procedural | Pass | The weakness is tested through repair steps rather than explained as a shortcut. |
| Outside pressure increases urgency | Pass | External interference forces the organization to act faster. |
| Antagonist remains competent | Needs revision | The current order risks feeling too convenient. Add a control condition or watcher. |
| Ning Zhao gains core access | Pass | Access follows from operational necessity. |
| Cost remains unresolved | Needs revision | Add a trace, suspicion, or system side effect before the chapter ends. |

## Canon Conflicts

- No direct canon conflict found.
- Watch the wording around the antagonist's authority. He should not sound careless or indulgent.

## Character Consistency

- Ning Zhao's technical caution is consistent.
- The antagonist's control need is present but should be sharpened in the order scene.
- Supporting staff should not become passive. Give one staff member a concrete objection or test.

## Knowledge State

- Ning Zhao must not know the full core procedure yet.
- The organization must not know Ning Zhao's deeper intent.
- The reader can know the immediate risk, but not the full future payoff.

## Hook Strength

Current hook: Ning Zhao enters the core area.

Recommended stronger hook:

```text
He enters the core area, but the repair log now contains one impossible timing mark.
Someone will audit it.
```

## Revision Recommendation

Revise before accepting the chapter.

Required fixes:

1. Add a control condition to the antagonist's order.
2. Add a visible cost or trace after the repair.
3. Make one supporting character resist or question the decision.
4. Keep rule explanation attached to Ning Zhao's next decision.
