# Sample Chapter Contract

This sample illustrates the kind of contract PlotRail asks the agent to prepare before prose drafting. It is based on the early arc of `假如我成为了反派`.

## Chapter

Chapter 8: controlled communication vulnerability.

## Purpose

Move Ning Zhao from an outer repair role toward the island's core area without making the antagonist look careless.

## Must Happen

- Ning Zhao identifies a communication weakness that can plausibly affect island operations.
- The weakness must be procedural and testable, not a convenient magic door.
- Outside-agent pressure increases the organization's anxiety.
- The antagonist orders Ning Zhao closer to the core area because the crisis requires a repairman with his observed skill.

## Must Not Happen

- Do not let the antagonist summon Ning Zhao for no reason.
- Do not reveal Ning Zhao's full identity or intent.
- Do not make the organization incompetent.
- Do not solve the communication weakness cleanly; leave a cost or unresolved consequence.

## Character State In

- Ning Zhao is cautious, technical, and still learning the island's power structure.
- The antagonist believes he controls the operating environment.
- Other agents treat Ning Zhao as a useful but low-status repairman.

## Character State Out

- Ning Zhao gains limited access to the core area.
- The antagonist sees him as more useful, not more trustworthy.
- The organization becomes more guarded because the vulnerability exposed an external pressure point.

## Reveals

- The communication system has a controlled but exploitable failure mode.
- The island's hierarchy responds quickly when infrastructure risk touches command stability.

## Secrets Preserved

- Ning Zhao's deeper agenda remains hidden.
- The full mechanism of body transfer remains unrevealed.
- The reader should not yet know every faction watching the island.

## Foreshadowing

- The repair action should create a later trace or suspicion.
- A secondary character should notice one detail that can matter in a later review.

## Hook Out

Ning Zhao reaches the core area, but the access comes with a new watcher and a procedure he cannot fully control.

## Continuity Risks

- If the antagonist appears too trusting, revise the order so it comes from operational necessity.
- If Ning Zhao succeeds too smoothly, add a failed test, fallback, or cost.
- If exposition slows the chapter, move rule explanation into a decision point.
